Anirudh Sharma

I lead Spatial AI at Amazon Lab126, exploring how intelligence can bridge digital and physical systems – making invisible bits of sensing smart using on-device CNNs and VLMs. I am a graduate and research affiliate of the Fluid Interfaces Group at the MIT Media Lab, where I worked with Pattie Maes on embedding gestural interfaces and intelligence into objects of everyday use. The goal was simple and radical at the same time: rather than pulling people into screens, bring computation into the world around them. What you see is what you cut, manipulate, and shape. I built and evaluated these novel civic and tangible interfaces, studying how physical interaction changes understanding.

My work has moved between research and deployment. I led human-centred AI at Useful Sensors, a spinout from the TensorFlow ecosystem at Google Brain, and earlier worked on gestural input and stroke recognition systems at Google and HP Labs. My research has appeared in ACM CHI, UIST, ICMI, and SIGGRAPH.

I have also built companies rooted in these ideas. I founded Ducere and created Lechal, India’s first wearable tech startup, developing haptic navigation footwear for the visually impaired, with more than 100,000 units shipped before exit in 2018. I later founded Graviky Labs, which captures carbon emissions from air pollution and converts them into AIR-INK, a carbon-based pigment used by over 10,000 artists and adopted by Stella McCartney, PANGAIA, Diageo’s Johnnie Walker, and Mastercard, and included in the Harvard Art Museums’ Forbes Pigment Collection.

Driven by social impact. Loves magic, tabla, bonfires, and unlikely collaborations across disciplines. If your work touches the physical world in unexpected ways, reach out.
