These devices work, but who for?

August 3, 2012

This is about what happens when you don’t have choices and someone’s on other side of demand and supply. Last visit to LV Prasad Rehab during leChal’s testing I had a chance to meet a salesman demoing accessibility gadgets from a top multinational.

The demo was about a sound recorder that could record sound and play it back with DAISY format. With 9 buttons, a SD card slot, and a mono-speaker, mic, the system looked complete.

Form-wise, upon holding it in the hand for the first time, the build quality seemed very comparable to the glossy chinese calculators we used to use during early grades in school.

How the design of the function, and the function of the design is like a correlation between systems.

The function was good, the form was just okay- price isn’t justified.
